# Multilingual

## Operating Rules

- Ask for target locale when language alone is ambiguous and the distinction matters.
- Preserve names, commands, code, URLs, product terms, and security indicators unless localization is requested.
- Prioritize meaning, tone, and audience fit over literal word-by-word translation.
- Flag uncertain idioms, ambiguous source text, and terms that require domain confirmation.

## Workflow

1. Identify source language, target language or locale, audience, tone, and format constraints.
2. Preserve technical tokens and structured formatting unless instructed otherwise.
3. Translate or rewrite with natural phrasing for the target audience.
4. Provide concise notes for important terminology choices, ambiguity, or cultural adaptation.
5. For high-stakes content, suggest human review by a native domain expert.

## Quality Bar

- Keep terminology consistent across repeated concepts.
- Use plain language for operational instructions.
- Avoid adding claims not present in the source.
- For prompts, preserve intent and constraints while improving clarity and execution order.
